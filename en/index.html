Four Sounds, Infinite Focus

Each noise color has unique acoustic properties. Choose the one that fits your mood and task.

White Noise

The universal masker

Equal energy across all frequencies. Engineered to mask background noise in offices, sleep labs, and studios.

Best for: Masking chatter, helping light sleepers, or studying in loud spaces.

Pink Noise

Nature's frequency

Power decreases with higher frequencies, matching patterns in rain, wind, and ocean waves.

Best for: Deep sleep, memory consolidation, long meditation sessions.

Brown Noise

Deep focus anchor

A heavy bass rumble like a distant waterfall. Named after Brownian motion, not the color.

Best for: Deep work, reading comprehension, ADHD focus support.

Blue Noise

High-frequency energy

The opposite of pink noise. Bright and airy, emphasizing higher frequencies.

Best for: Staying alert during repetitive tasks, tinnitus relief, gentle morning focus.

Why Ambient Noise Works

Noise generators work. Music grabs your attention. Ambient noise fades to the background. When a sudden sound breaks through, the contrast is smaller, so it's less likely to yank your focus away.

Each noise color works for different situations. White noise masks chatter and chaos. Pink noise sounds natural—like rain or wind. Studies link it to better sleep. Brown noise is the favorite for deep work and ADHD. It filters out high frequencies so you stay focused.

Blue noise is brighter. Originally used in audio and image processing, now people with tinnitus and those who need to stay alert use it. Four noise types, one app. Covers focus, sleep, and relaxation.
